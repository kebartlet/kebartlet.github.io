K.E. Bartlet (KEH-ih-ee-BAHR-tliht; born January 1998) is an American engineer, technology professor, and author. She is best known for writing Political Techno Thriller, Science Fiction, and Romance.

First conceived as an academic paper on the fragile state of the South Korean Military reserve system in the face of North Korean aggression, she converted that premise into a full length fiction novel, My Dumpling Your Dumpling, and published it in January 2025.

From there, she went on a roll and published the sequel, Operation Danger Close, in May 2025, set in a fictionalized 2025 and with a new presidential regime in power, loosely modeled after the crises plaguing the free world at the time of publication, but with an incredibly unexpected ending.

Although an author of primarily fiction works, Bartlet is an engineer by virtue of education and experience (with over a decade of experience across Big Tech (multiple FAANGs), BioTech, Pharma, the U.S. Government, and higher education) and this reflects in her work, both fiction and non-fiction.

Early life and education

K.E. was born in 1998 in Mountain View, California, down the street from what would become Google later that year, to two "Preacher's Kids", one an engineer and the other an accountant. Because of their long working hours, up until she entered pre-school, she was largely raised by her paternal grandparents, immigrants from South Korea with limited English proficiency. This resulted in her first language being Korean despite having been born in the United States, not learning English until preschool, at which point she proceeded to forget all of her Korean.

In what she later found to be an interesting origin story for an author, she initially disliked to read and write. It wasn't until a wonderful second grade teacher named Tiffanie Moyano unlocked a passion for reading that the journey that led her to becoming an author began. By the age of 9, she had run out of Hardy Boys and Nancy Drew books to read and in a fit of boredom, plucked a Tom Clancy novel off of a bookshelf, diving right into the Cold War era geopolitical spy thriller even without adequate knowledge of the broader world in which the story occurred.

Through the remainder of her childhood, she continued to be an avid "bookworm", whose behavior was controlled by the frequently used threat of "If you don't do (insert demand), you won't get to go to the library." This paired excellently with a natural curiosity, a spirit best characterized as "I wonder if I could get X to do Y."

At age 12, she moved to Cupertino, California, home to the global headquarters for Apple, which she lived less than 4 miles from. It was while living there that she experienced ridicule from her peers for not having a Mac, which drove her to building a Hackintosh on a Windows computer. She was scared that she would mess up the process and render the computer itself unusable, which was why she built her first virtual machine before her teenage years started, an accomplishment she later brought up while interviewing for a Principal Security Engineer role at VMware, the company that made the virtualization software she had used. While doing so, she had to learn about EFI security in order to get the Mac operating system to boot up, which started a lifelong interest in cybersecurity, which she later turned into her profession as a hacker.

Experiencing frustrations with the "locked down" nature of the Apple environment (at the time iOS and Mac OS X), at age 13, she ventured out to the Android world with the Samsung Galaxy Player 5.0, which inspired a short lived stint as a software engineer, coding new versions of the Android Operating System (including CyanogenMod, later LineageOS, and Paranoid Android)

It was after this period of exploration into various technologies that would later accelerate her budding technology career that she experienced a more tumultuous part of her formative years. Amidst struggling with her sexual orientation and gender identity in an America that was still slowly beginning to come to terms with its LGBTQ members, and the challenges of being different in a household environment that demanded conformity, she experienced periods of homelessness that eventually resulted in her being officially placed in the foster care system, where she bounced from placement to placement until aging out of foster care, during which time she also managed to graduate high school early at the age of 16.

Career

At the young age of 17, after graduating high school as a 16 year old, K.E. started her career working full time in the Silicon Valley high tech industry as an IT Engineer & field CIO for a Managed Service Provider. In that role, she handled the IT needs of various high tech client companies, often tasked with IT infrastructure deployments and managing security matters for clients ranging from medical institutions to biotechnology startups to manufacturing companies. During this same period, she began working her way through her undergraduate degree at Southern New Hampshire University, selected primarily for its affordability and compatibility with the 60 to 80 hour weeks she found herself working.

As she progressed through the field, often working two or more jobs at the same time, both to accelerate her career and to build financial security in one of the highest cost of living metropolitan areas in the world, she found herself working in progressively more senior engineering roles at Apple at age 20, Facebook at age 22, and more as she worked her way up the tech industry's ladder. During this same period of time, she also found herself working in various public sector roles across various levels of government and areas of responsibility.

She also began to develop an budding entrepreneurial nature, founding her own consultancy, creating a banner under which she and other high tech professionals could moonlight more effectively. Under this umbrella, she led a diverse team of professionals in offering an excellent business value proposition for their clients, handling matters ranging from modernizing their technology environments to handling major security incidents to advising on the intersection of technology and finance among other high value services.

At the age of 23, she also started teaching in the higher education setting as an adjunct professor and later guest lecturer, forging a path through various universities with industry credentials in quickly evolving technical fields that negated any concerns about her youth. This also led to her working with the technology industry certifying body CompTIA as one of its Subject Matter Experts, helping set the standards and write the certifying exams for the next generation of technology industry practitioners, an experience she found particularly enjoyable compared to even the best highlights of her diverse career, getting to eat great food while arguing with other incredibly intelligent technology professionals.

Educational Content Creation

As part of her commitment to democratizing access to knowledge, K.E. Bartlet is a contributor to technology and finance content produced by Hacker-Fu Brands .

Creative Community

As her writing adventure built up steam, K.E. realized just how great it was to have a community of like minded creative folks. So along with artist and author Anna Marie, she created the Characters, Spaces, Rainbows online creative community. The community offers free and low cost support to authors, artists, designers, and other creative professionals in the domains of editing, publishing, design, and marketing, functioning as an ad hoc publishing company, all without the gatekeeping of traditional publishing houses.

Personal Life

When she's not tackling the technology world or busy writing her various works, K.E. Bartlet still enjoys reading, including works from some of her favorite authors below:

  • Tom Clancy
  • Lily Seabrooke
  • Andrew Watts
  • James Rosone / Miranda Watson
  • Monica McCallan
  • Jacqueline Ramsden

As is evident in her various fiction works, she greatly enjoys food. When she discusses her travels with friends and family, the most often related experiences surround the food she consumed on her travels, not any other aspect of her journey.

She also began a Digital Nomad journey in October 2022, living the "have laptop, will travel" location independent lifestyle, except for when interrupted by superseding commitments. When not traveling, she is based out of the Pacific Northwest, calling a little town outside of Seattle, Washington home. As of August 2025, she has been to 43 countries and 2 non-sovereign / non-recognized territories.

K.E. Bartlet is also an out and proud member of the LGBTQ community (not a bank), a lived experience to which she attributes significant impact on her work.

Thank you for reading this far.

If you've read this far, congratulations. You get to know that K.E. Bartlet is a nom de plume. A pen name.

All the other details remain accurate, but in addition to being a veteran cybersecurity professional who values her privacy, she started her publishing journey before the severing of her remaining affiliations with the United States Government was complete.

So to avoid any issues with regulations governing political activity (which she interpreted to include publishing a novel with significant political themes), she published under her nom de plume, and once she started publishing under that name, it became easier to keep publishing under that name. Now that she no longer has any relationship with the Big Green Weenie of Uncle Sam, she does engage with some of her readers and fans as her true self.

For anyone who's interested, this is where the name comes from:

K = Katherine, after Katherine Goble Johnson, an American mathematician who helped the free world win the space race. She was played by Taraji P. Henson in the 2016 biographical drama film Hidden Figures, which is one of K.E. Bartlet's favorite movies.

E = Elizabeth, after Secretary of State Elizabeth McCord, the main protagonist from the American TV series Madam Secretary (2014-2019), an apolitical and pragmatic maverick of a Secretary of State who is depicted as just wanting things to work.

Bartlet comes from President Josiah Edward "Jed" Bartlet from the American TV series The West Wing (1999-2006), which despite being incredibly idealized is often hailed as a love letter to public service. "If only we could have a President like that again in the United States, but this time for real instead of on our screens." is a sentiment often expressed by K.E. Bartlet.

And as to why K.E. instead of Katherine E. or Katherine Elizabeth, she deliberately chose to use initials to combat any internalized misogyny that might deter a reader in the political techno thriller genre from considering her books, instead opting to let the quality of the works themselves stand on their own, a common practice by female authors throughout history.
